# Compare Page Overrides

## Result hierarchy

- Comparison outcomes must be identifiable at a glance through icon, label, border and colour together.
- Positive: check icon, explicit “Equivalenti ufficiali” text and the semantic `success` token.
- Negative: X icon, explicit “Non equivalenti ufficiali” text and the semantic `destructive` token.
- Never use red or green alone: the wording and icon remain the primary meaning carriers.
- Give the result container a visible left border and a low-intensity semantic surface; preserve readable contrast in light and dark mode.

## Research basis

- IED frames effective UX around intuitive interaction that accounts for people’s needs; its interface-design material highlights colour, hierarchy, placement and language as behavioural signals.
- WCAG 2.2 SC 1.4.1 requires information not to be conveyed by colour alone, while SC 1.4.11 requires meaningful non-text UI elements to retain sufficient contrast.
